What Antarctica Taught Me

"I applied for this because it was different to the average MBA. It wasn’t about the individual, it wasn’t about finance, it wasn’t about markets and profits. It was about using science to make decisions, communicating the science, and skillset that STEM women might need to make their voices heard. Then above that, the building of a network that could drive change. My key learnings were: Visibility: Really not something that I was comfortable with, or saw the need for particularly in industry. The Value angle I think was really useful to draw the distinction between noise and purpose to visibility. LSI: I took a lot from this one. Firstly on seeing where some of my brain energy is being spent in conflict bowties etc on LSI1, then seeing that on LSI2 I’m not passing them on. Really good insight in what to do / not do / carry on doing from a behaviour standpoint. Doing the “perfect” leader LSI I also found useful to put almost a normalisation on the scoring? Before that it felt more like personal criticism, but with a perfect leader in hand, there was a point of reference.. The stories & community: I’ve never been one for large groups of friends or shoulders to cry on. But there is definitely something in the sharing of stories that in a corporate environment NEVER happens. The authentic sharing I think really opened up the learning environment much more than I’ve seen in other courses. Yes, people share stories in other courses, but they’re usually metaphors rather than true painful personal stories. So the reality of life came across for me."

Karen

Spenley
