What Antarctica Taught Me

"The program gave me a unique opportunity to question myself about my core values in an environment where my own voice was able to be heard by myself, a chance to, for a moment step outside of expectations, expectations of what my values should be from society or my family and ask myself what do I personally value above everything else. Something I had not done before. This allowed me to analyse where I was spending my time and energy. Was I giving energy to things that did not fit my values and so in the end were not helpful or not bringing me happiness and allowed to explore how I might better use these limited resources on things that deeply resonate with my values, goals and passions.

Homeward Bound also taught me the importance of collaboration and networks, not just formal professional networks, but ones in which one can relate to and discuss both personal and professional challenges to a group that have the power to actively listen, provide advice that can be trusted to be in the best interest of those seeking help and to challenge each other to step outside of our comfort zones more readily safe in the knowledge that support is available.

Finally, the importance of representation and diversity to both understand and solve the multifaceted problems we face globally is absolutely key to successful progress for society and the planet."

Alexandra

Leeper
