What Antarctica Taught Me

"On its surface homeward bound is global leadership program for women in STEMM. What made the program such an impactfully experience was not directly the content, although that was excellent. The program was a 4-way interaction, of which only the first part was directly learning about leadership. The second part is being “off the grid” for three weeks to deep dive into ourselves relative to leadership. This is the core of the program, where the basis of strong leadership is knowing oneself so that one can lead from the basis of our values. The program is about getting space away from the distractions of everyday life to think about what one’s core values are, and whether the life we are living are in alignment with these. The third part is the incredible women who are part of the program. They are mentors, peers, like-minded, and some of the most incredible women who I have met. Lastly is the continent of Antarctica, from which I learned how one can be both fragile and strong. The program has made me comfortable with the idea that leadership is not always the person standing at the front/top who is the only leader, but that leadership can be where you use the skills you have is the way that will help best. In the same way, you can lead anywhere, whether it is within your local group, on the world stage, to your children, nieces etc. One final point is how it still amazes me that when they asked the room who felt as if they had “imposter syndrome” to be on the program, 1005 of women in the world put their hands up. Wow."

Bec

Handcock
